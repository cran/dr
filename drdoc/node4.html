Sliced inverse regression

Sliced inverse regression, or sir, was proposed by Li (1991); see Cook (1998, Chapter 11). In sir, we make use of the fact that given certain assumptions on the marginal distribution of 1, the inverse regression problem . The general computational outline for sir is as follows:
  1. Examine by dividing the range of into slices, each with approximately the same number of observations. With a multivariate response ( has columns), divide the range of into cells. For example, when , and we slice into 3 slices, into 2 slices, and into 4 slices, we will have cells. The number of slices or cells is a tuning parameter of the procedure.
  2. Assume that within each slice or cell is approximately constant. Then the expected value of the within-slice vector of sample means will be a vector in .
  3. Form the matrix whose -th row is the vector of weighted sample means in the -th slice. The matrix is the sample covariance matrix of these sample mean vectors.
